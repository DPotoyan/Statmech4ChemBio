# Quiz: Phase Transitions and the Ising Model

---

### Question 1

What are the two main types of phase transitions? For each type, give one physical example and state whether the order parameter changes continuously or discontinuously at the transition.

---

### Question 2

Write down the Ising model Hamiltonian and define every symbol in it. What physical assumption does the notation $\langle ij \rangle$ encode?

---

### Question 3

Explain the Peierls argument for why the 1D Ising model has **no** phase transition at any finite temperature. Your answer should include the free energy cost of creating a domain wall.

---

### Question 4

In the 2D Ising model, the Peierls argument gives an estimate for the critical temperature. Sketch the key steps of the argument and state the resulting expression for $T_c$. How does it compare to Onsager's exact result?

---

### Question 5

The magnetic susceptibility can be written as

$$\chi(T) = \frac{1}{k_B T}\left[\langle M^2 \rangle - \langle M \rangle^2\right]$$

Interpret this equation: what does it tell you about the connection between **fluctuations** and **response**? What happens to $\chi$ as $T \to T_c$?

---

### Question 6

Near the critical point, thermodynamic quantities follow power laws. Fill in the table with the correct power-law expression for each quantity.

| Quantity | Symbol | Power law |
|---|---|---|
| Order parameter | $m$ | ? |
| Susceptibility | $\chi$ | ? |
| Heat capacity | $C_v$ | ? |
| Correlation length | $\xi$ | ? |

---

### Question 7

What is **universality** in the context of phase transitions? What microscopic properties determine which universality class a system belongs to? Give two physically different systems that belong to the same universality class.

---

### Question 8

The correlation function $c(r)$ measures how spin fluctuations at two sites are related. How does $c(r)$ decay far from the critical point? What qualitatively changes at $T = T_c$, and why does this have such dramatic consequences for the system's behavior?

---

### Question 9

The transfer matrix method gives the exact partition function of the 1D Ising model as $Z = \lambda_+^N + \lambda_-^N$. Explain briefly why the analyticity of $\lambda_+$ for all finite $T$ rules out a phase transition in 1D.

---

### Question 10

The Ising model was originally invented for ferromagnetism, but it has been applied to many other systems. Describe **two** non-magnetic systems where an Ising-like model captures the essential physics. In each case, identify what plays the role of the "spin" variable.

---

### Bonus Question

The Rushbrooke scaling relation states $\alpha + 2\beta + \gamma = 2$. What deeper theoretical framework explains why critical exponents are not all independent? Why was this framework worth a Nobel Prize?
